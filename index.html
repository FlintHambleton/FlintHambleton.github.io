How I Built My Electric Go-Kart

Step-by-step instructions for assembling a 72 V electric go-kart using a 5000 W BLDC motor kit. No background story, just what to buy, how to mount it, how to wire it, and how to do first tests.

Safety checklist

This build uses a high-power battery pack. Always:

  • Work with an experienced adult.
  • Use insulated tools and proper fuses.
  • Wear eye protection and gloves.
  • Test off the ground before riding.

If you are not sure about any step, stop and ask someone who knows high-voltage DC systems.

3. Mechanical setup

Install the motor, chain drive, and battery mounts before touching wiring. Keep all high-power parts low and centered for stability.

Step 3.1
Mount the motor

Use the motor’s mounting slots or plate to attach it firmly to the frame.

  1. Position the motor near the rear axle on the same side as the sprocket.
  2. Align the motor sprocket with the axle sprocket as straight as possible.
  3. Mark bolt locations on the frame, then drill the mounting holes.
  4. Bolt the motor down with grade-8 hardware and lock nuts. Leave a small amount of adjustment room for chain tension.

Tips

Keep at least one slot or slotted bracket so you can slide the motor forward/backward to tension the chain.

Step 3.2
Install chain & sprockets
  1. Install the rear #35 sprocket on the axle if it is not already mounted.
  2. Wrap the chain around both sprockets and mark the correct length with 1–2 links of slack.
  3. Use a chain breaker or grinder to cut it to length.
  4. Re-join the chain with a master link. Rotate the axle slowly to check that the chain runs straight and doesn’t bind.
  5. Slide the motor to tension the chain: there should be a small amount of vertical play (roughly 10–15 mm at the longest span).
Step 3.3
Mount the battery and controller
  1. Choose a spot in the center of the frame, low and protected from debris, for the battery pack.
  2. Build a box or tray for the pack using steel angle or thick plywood. Strap it down tightly with metal straps or ratchet straps.
  3. Mount the controller on a metal plate with airflow. Keep it close to both the motor and battery to minimize cable length.
  4. Make sure nothing can hit the battery or controller when the steering is turned or the suspension moves.

4. High-voltage wiring

Follow the wiring diagram from your motor kit exactly. This section shows the general order and layout used in the build.

Step 4.1
Plan the power path

Layout the main power wiring before cutting anything. The typical order is:

Battery + → Fuse → Switch/Breaker → Controller +
Controller − → Battery −

  1. Disconnect the battery while planning. Never work with a live pack.
  2. Mark cable routes along the frame where they will be protected and tied down.
  3. Avoid sharp edges and moving parts. Use grommets where cables pass through metal.
Step 4.2
Install fuse and main switch
  1. Mount the ANL fuse holder or DC breaker within 15–20 cm of the battery positive terminal.
  2. Cut a short cable from battery + to the input of the fuse or breaker and crimp lugs on both ends.
  3. Cut a second cable from the output of the fuse/breaker to the controller + terminal.
  4. Install the fuse but leave it removed or switched off until final testing.
Step 4.3
Connect controller and motor
  1. Connect the controller’s three phase outputs (usually labeled U/V/W or A/B/C) to the motor’s three phase wires with the matching color pairs from the kit.
  2. Connect the controller’s main negative (B−) directly to the battery negative using heavy cable.
  3. Tighten all studs with lock washers and nuts. Cover exposed terminals with heat-shrink or boots.

Hall sensors & other motor leads

Plug the motor’s hall sensor connector into the matching controller plug from the kit. Do not cut or swap individual hall wires unless the manual tells you to.

Step 4.4
Low-voltage wiring (throttle & key)
  1. Mount the throttle on the steering wheel or pedal according to the kit instructions.
  2. Plug the throttle connector into the controller’s throttle input harness as labeled in the controller manual.
  3. Wire the key switch in series with the controller’s low-voltage enable wire (often labeled “ignition”, “on/off”, or “lock”). This lets the key turn the controller on and off.
  4. Zip-tie all small wires away from the chain and tires.

5. Setup and first tests

Bring the system up slowly and keep the rear wheels off the ground until everything works correctly.

Step 5.1
Pre-power checks
  1. Lift the rear of the kart on sturdy stands so the wheels spin freely.
  2. Verify that the chain clears the frame and brake rotor.
  3. Use a multimeter to confirm correct battery voltage at the pack terminals.
  4. With the fuse still removed or breaker open, check that there is no short between battery + and battery −.
Step 5.2
Initial power-on
  1. Insert the main fuse or close the breaker while the key switch is off.
  2. Turn the key to “on”. The controller should power up (LED or app depending on the model).
  3. Gently roll on a small amount of throttle and watch the rear wheels spin. If they spin in reverse, use the controller settings or swap any two motor phase wires.
  4. Listen for grinding, chain noise, or rubbing and fix anything before riding.
Step 5.3
On-ground testing
  1. Lower the kart back to the ground.
  2. Verify the mechanical brakes work perfectly before applying power.
  3. Start with short, slow passes in an open area with an adult supervising. Gradually increase throttle only after you are sure everything is stable.
